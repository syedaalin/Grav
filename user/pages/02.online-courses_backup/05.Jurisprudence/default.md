## Course Overview

This comprehensive Fiqh course provides students with a thorough grounding in Shia Islamic jurisprudence, from the methodology of deriving rulings to practical applications in daily life. Learn from classical texts and contemporary scholars to navigate religious obligations with confidence.

### What You'll Learn

- **Usul al-Fiqh (Principles of Jurisprudence)**
  - Sources of Islamic law (Quran, Sunnah, Aql, Ijma)
  - Principles of interpretation
  - Role of Mujtahid and Taqlid
  - Understanding legal terminology

- **Acts of Worship (Ibadat)**
  - Taharah (Purification)
  - Salat (Prayer) - detailed rulings
  - Sawm (Fasting)
  - Hajj and Ziyarat
  - Khums and Zakat

- **Transactions (Muamalat)**
  - Business and trade
  - Marriage and divorce
  - Inheritance
  - Islamic banking and finance
  - Modern contracts

- **Contemporary Issues**
  - Medical ethics (organ donation, IVF, etc.)
  - Technology and social media
  - Environmental Islamic law
  - Gender and family issues
  - International relations

### Methodology

Our approach combines:
- Classical texts (Tahrir al-Wasilah, Islamic Laws by Ayatullah Sistani)
- Comparative Fiqh across maraji
- Case studies and real-world scenarios
- Q&A sessions with qualified scholars
- Research projects on contemporary issues

### Prerequisites

- Basic knowledge of Islamic beliefs
- Familiarity with Arabic terms (helpful but not required)
- Commitment to weekly readings and assignments

### Course Benefits

✅ **Practical Knowledge** - Apply Fiqh in your daily life
